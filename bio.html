🚀 A.P.J Abdul Kalam

Known as the 'Missile Man of India', Dr. A.P.J Abdul Kalam was a brilliant scientist and the 11th President of India. He played a pivotal role in India’s space and missile programs, especially in ISRO and DRDO. He inspired millions with his humility, wisdom, and vision for youth empowerment.

×

✌️ Mahatma Gandhi

Mohandas Karamchand Gandhi led India’s freedom movement through non-violence and civil disobedience. Revered as the 'Father of the Nation', his philosophy of Satyagraha inspired peace movements across the world.

×

🧱 Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel

Sardar Patel played a crucial role in uniting 562 princely states post-Independence. Known as the 'Iron Man of India', his leadership and administrative skill helped lay the foundation of modern India’s unity and integrity.

×

⚔️ Subhash Chandra Bose

A fearless leader, Bose founded the Indian National Army (INA) to liberate India from British rule. His slogan “Give me blood and I will give you freedom” still resonates with patriotic zeal. He is honored for his revolutionary courage and nationalism.

×

🎤 Sarojini Naidu

Known as the 'Nightingale of India', Sarojini Naidu was a poet, freedom fighter, and the first woman governor of independent India. Her eloquence and contribution to women’s rights and Indian politics were remarkable.
